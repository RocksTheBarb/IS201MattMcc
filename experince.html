My Experience

I was born with a fire that drives me

Life prepares us for the road ahead.

If one only takes time to observe.

  • When I was 15 years old, I took a look at my life. I was tired of using others to get what I wanted. So, as all must do when facing independence, we must pursue a job. I began my working life as an assistant at a local construction company and, for the next summer, developed many skills I still use.

    I learned hard skills like putting up siding, framing, adding trim, renovating, installing windows, etc. But I also learned invaluable soft skills. such as communication with superiors, working long hard hours, time management, and most importantly, listening.

  • This came in handy in school as I graduated high school with over a 4.0 weighted GPA and 4 credit hours of college completed. While in school, I won 3 art competitions and created 2 personal courses and curriculums in art.

  • My next job was working at Taco Bell as a kitchen staff. Taking my serious nature of self-improvement further, I quickly learned all the tasks, from making orders to taking them. I never slacked or sat around, which gained the respect of my supervisors.

    With these skills in customer service and food prep, I returned after a two-year mission and was quickly trained as a Shift lead. I gained some hard skills like food preparation, but the soft skills are where true growth was. I gained further team leadership and de-escalation training; I learned how to manage inventory and banking information, and I acquired some skills to run a successful business.

  • I, as a man of faith, felt to put my money where my mouth is and serve a religious mission for my God and my church. This was a two-year-long mission where I was sent wherever the church felt like I was needed. I started in south Utah due to COVID-19 and made many great connections in the business world there. I spent fifteen months all over Saint George, Utah, Arizona, and Nevada before getting sent to Paris, France. While overseas, I had the chance to learn to read, write, and speak French at a B2 CEFR level. I also lead teams of missionaries in Belgium in marketing and community outreach! While away from family, I learned to be self-sufficient, self-motivating, deadline and goal-oriented, and a team player. I learned interpersonal skills like cold contacts and teaching skills. Even when people didn't want to hear our message, I learned how to befriend them and learn about who they are. As a missionary, I was also tasked with confidential information handling and was able to pass a visa clearance background check.

  • After coming home, I started college and had to get another job to help pay the bills. This led me down a route of working as an Amazon XL driver for a few months, giving me access to a commercial license and time management skills as routes were up to me to adjust and plan around.

  • I then used Amazon and my driving skills to get a high-security clearance job as an air ramp personnel. This involved training as a Deicer, tug driver, belt load operator and driver, and plane loading. I also worked in the warehouse and gained 6 hours of forklift on-the-job training. I am also fully certified to perform marshelling on aircraft and ground support equipment.

  • I am attending BYU as a pre-business major, having taken a class in information systems that has allowed me to learn VBA, SQL, HTML, CSS, Tableau, and more, which I discuss on the skills page.
